# Global Conflict v2 — Market Research & Feature Recommendations

*Companion to `CITY_DESIGN_OVERVIEW.md`. Synthesized from five parallel research passes (idle/clickers, mobile city builders, MMO player economies, collective-governance games, F2P retention benchmarks), June 2026. Claims below carry source links and a confidence note where evidence is weak.*

---

## 1. Verdict in one paragraph

The v2 direction is sound: the pivot from zero-sum missiles to a build/trade/vote economy matches what every durable comparable did, and the shared-city + voting layer is genuinely the design's strongest asset (social features are the best-evidenced D30+ retention lever in F2P). But the research surfaced three structural problems the doc doesn't address: **(1)** a clicker with a capped click rate and *no offline/idle progression* contradicts the genre's main retention engine; **(2)** paid multipliers that also multiply *votes* means payers buy governance of a shared entity — the one mechanic with no successful precedent and strong backlash precedent; **(3)** removing missiles removes what would have been the economy's best structural resource **sink**, and click-faucet economies without sinks reliably inflate. All three are fixable without changing the pitch.

## 2. Fact-check of the doc's claims

| Doc's claim | Verdict | What the evidence says |
|---|---|---|
| SimCity BuildIt: "happiness drives population and income" | **Partly right** | Happiness sets the **tax/income bracket** (0% → 20% at ≥90% happiness); population is gated by **housing + the crafted materials needed to upgrade it**, not by happiness. "Unhappy citizens leave" is weakly supported. Doc's §3 should say: happiness → income & click effectiveness; housing + production → population. ([EA guide](https://help.ea.com/en/articles/simcity/simcity-buildit/beginner-guide/), [Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/progression-control-in-sim-city-buildit)) |
| "Housing capacity gates population" (SCBI) | **Verified** | Core SCBI engine; safe to copy. |
| SCBI "top-grossing for years" | **Historically true, not current** | Headline EA Mobile earner ~2015–2017, ~5th-highest EA mobile lifetime; now a long tail (~$6.5k/day est.). Fine as precedent, don't oversell. |
| "eRepublik showed an unconstrained player economy ends in crises" | **Right outcome, wrong cause** | eRepublik's economy did die and players left (~400k → ~186k around the V2 era). But the documented killers were **(a)** unpoliced bots/multis flooding markets and **(b)** "Work as Manager" — a *developer-introduced* zero-marginal-cost production loop that caused a deflationary glut. Free player pricing was not the culprit. Lesson: police multis and never ship zero-marginal-cost production — not "cap prices". ([eRepublik wiki: Inflation](https://wiki.erepublik.com/index.php/Inflation), [forum postmortem](https://forum.erepublik.com/index.php?%2Ftopic%2F3030-options-for-a-better-economy-module%2Fpage-1=)) |
| Price bands as MVP guardrail | **Precedented, with a caveat** | OGame and Travian enforce trade-ratio bands — but as **anti-collusion/anti-multi** tools, enforced by bans. No major free-market MMO uses price ceilings. SCBI's hard price caps **destroyed price signaling** (everything sells at max). If bands ship, make them wide and treat them as abuse detection, not price stabilizers; prefer **transaction fees/taxes** (EVE's model) as the durable guardrail. |
| "Collective decision-making alone has kept NationStates players engaged for two decades" | **Overstated** | NationStates is 20+ years old, but its creator credits the **community** (regions, roleplay, player-invented raiding/defending), not the voting mechanics: "I made a site where you could create a nation and talk to people. The community did everything else." Voting without a social substrate is a daily poll, not a community. ([Max Barry, 20th anniversary](https://maxbarry.com/2022/11/12/news.html)) |
| War Clicks sells autoclickers / tolerates automation | **Verified** | War Clicks ships a built-in autoclicker as a progression feature and advises capping external autoclickers at its 10 clicks/sec limit. Clicker Heroes also sells Auto Clickers for premium currency. Automation-tolerance is genre-normal. ([War Clicks dev blog](https://blog.warclicks.com/autoclicker-feature-added/), [Clicker Heroes wiki](https://clickerheroes.fandom.com/wiki/Rubies)) |
| Cookie Clicker / AdCap as pay-for-convenience precedent | **Verified, doesn't transfer cleanly** | True in single-player. In a *competitive shared world*, time-compression purchases are literal pay-to-win — the configuration that produced the Diablo Immortal / Lost Ark backlashes. The cap makes it worse: with autoclickers tolerated, every active player converges on the cap, so **the only differentiator left is the paid multiplier** (Popcat's 800/30s cap produced exactly this: an ecosystem of scripts at 799/30s). |
| Virtonomics as a durability proof | **Weak — drop it** | Alive since 2006 but "four nearly dead servers", dead community channels, ~$20/mo aggressive monetization. A surviving niche product, not a model. Cite EVE/Albion instead. |
| Real-world geography as the board | **Engaging hook, weak moat** | Pokémon GO shows location pride drives engagement but churn stays high; Parallel Kingdom died; Atlas Earth monetizes but via widely-criticized dark patterns. Geography is identity glue ("we're making Rotterdam an energy hub"), not retention by itself. |

## 3. Features to adopt (prioritized)

### P0 — fix the MVP before launch

1. **An idle/offline production layer.** The single most damning gap. Idle games hit ~18% DAU/MAU stickiness vs 10.5% for hyper-casual and ~9x ARPDAU precisely because progress accrues offline (Kongregate/GDC data). Concretely: buildings staffed by population produce a trickle *while you're away*; clicks act as the **active multiplier on top** (operate a building = burst of output). This also makes population-as-workforce mechanically real, and the "what did my city do overnight" check-in is the appointment hook. Every durable clicker (Cookie Clicker, AdCap, Clicker Heroes, Realm Grinder, Melvor, War Clicks) added an automation/idle layer; pure-active clickers are viral fads that decay (Popcat).
2. **Decouple votes from paid multipliers.** Sell multipliers on *production* only; votes accrue per active day or per capped contribution, or use quadratic/capped weighting. Evidence: contribution-weighted governance converges on plutocracy (top 15–20% of DAO token holders control ~78% of vote power); selling power into shared systems is the most reliable backlash trigger in F2P (Diablo Immortal, Lost Ark). This is the doc's open question #2 — the research answer is firmly "capped weight, never purchasable."
3. **Economy sinks, designed in from day one.** Clicks are an unbounded faucet; UO's collapse → the faucet-drain model is the canonical lesson, and even EVE hit stagflation in 2020-21 despite fees. At MVP: market transaction fee + listing fee (cash sink), escalating construction/upgrade costs (resource sink), goods consumed by population needs (the needs system *is* a sink — size it deliberately), optional building decay/maintenance. Publish a simple per-city "economic report" (EVE's Monthly Economic Report pattern builds trust and is cheap — the data is already in SQLite).
4. **Per-city chat or message board + city identity.** NationStates' actual retention engine. Without a social substrate the poll is a button, not a community. A scrolling city feed (who clicked, who built, who voted, market trades) is the minimum; chat is better. The existing WebSocket hub already broadcasts per-city events — this is infrastructure the codebase nearly has.
5. **Personal contribution recognition inside the shared city.** Free-riding is the default in public-goods settings; Foxhole's logistics strike shows uncredited collective labor breeds revolt. Per-city contributor leaderboard ("top builders of Rotterdam this week"), name-on-the-building for top contributors to a construction, personal lifetime stats. Cheap, and it's the bridge between v1's personal achievements (which carry over) and v2's shared property.
6. **Anti-multi enforcement.** eRepublik's actual killer #1. Cookie-UUID auth with no passwords makes multis trivial — one person can register N accounts and capture a small city's poll. At minimum: per-IP heuristics, vote weight requiring account age/activity, and the price-band machinery repurposed as anomaly detection.

### P1 — first post-launch quarter

7. **Daily quests + login streak.** Cheapest D7 lever with the most consistent vendor data (7-day streaks ≈ ~25% more DAU retained at D30; academic work confirms the lift, warns of "feels like a job" — keep it to 2-3 tasks). Tasks write themselves from existing mechanics: "operate the power plant 50 times, place a market order, cast your vote."
8. **Weekly city league / co-op cadence.** The proven city-builder retention loop is a weekly rhythm: SCBI's Contest of Mayors (7-day league of 100 similar players), Township's Regatta (weekly co-op task board), Forge of Empires' Guild Expeditions (auto-starts every Tuesday). For v2: a weekly **city-vs-city league** on growth/happiness/trade volume — this is also the "city-vs-city competition without missiles" the doc defers, and it slots into the existing daily-snapshot machinery (`snapshots.go`).
9. **A re-engagement channel.** Web push or email ("your construction finished", "the poll closes in 2h", "Rotterdam was overtaken"). Games with push show ~3x retention vs none (correlational but directionally safe). The codebase has no channel at all today; cookie-only auth means an optional email field is also account recovery — two birds.
10. **Dead-city handling.** Industry-standard, and the doc's open question #3: Wurm Online garbage-collects lapsed settlements; Clash of Clans auto-rotates inactive leaders after 21–35 days. For v2: cities with no active residents decay gently (happiness drifts down, no production), and offer **free migration** to residents of stagnant cities. A player alone in a small town is fine as a *choice* only if leaving is cheap.

### P2 — later, with evidence gates

11. **Seasons / battle pass** — now standard (60%+ of top-grossing mobile; SCBI's Mayor's Pass at $4.99), but it's a monetization tool more than a retention tool (the one peer-reviewed study found spending lift, no engagement lift) and it needs content sinks the MVP won't have. Ship only once events exist.
12. **Timed live-ops events** — AdCap's postmortem credits live events for sustaining 1M+ DAU; top-grossing games keep adding them. Needs tooling; don't hand-build at MVP.
13. **Transport tiers with capacity and risk, not just delay.** Screeps (the closest precedent to distance-cost trading between player territories) found cost-only friction produces dull neighbor-only trade; EVE's hauling is engaging because of *risk*. Flat delay at MVP is right; when tiers come, add capacity limits and something at stake.
14. **Prestige-equivalent, optional.** Proven (Cookie Clicker's Ascension) but **not required** — Melvor Idle explicitly rejects prestige and retains via content breadth, and a shared persistent city is awkward to reset. A *personal* prestige (career/legacy track per player, not per city) fits better if late-game pacing needs it. Low priority.

## 4. Features to avoid or change

- **Don't sell vote power** (covered above — the one no-precedent mechanic in the doc).
- **Don't use hard price caps as the market guardrail.** SCBI's caps killed price discovery; Hay Day caps at 3.6× default and its market degenerated similarly. Wide bands as abuse-detection + transaction fees as the real stabilizer. Also worth noting: **Township passed $1B lifetime with no player-to-player trading at all** — the market is a differentiator, not a retention requirement, so don't let it block launch.
- **Don't ship any zero-marginal-cost production path** (free building operation with no input/energy cost) — eRepublik's "Work as Manager" deflation spiral.
- **Don't rely on "scripting tolerated + cap" as stated.** Precedent exists (War Clicks), but combined with paid multipliers it collapses into pure pay-to-win at the cap. The idle layer (P0 #1) defuses this: when baseline production is idle and clicks are a boost, the cap stops being the whole game. Keep the visible meter — that part is right (silent drops do read as bugs).
- **Don't remove conflict forever without replacing the sink.** Missiles were a resource/cash sink and an emotional stake. The doc's open question #1 — the evidence-based answer: remove them at MVP, but plan the later conflict layer as **economic warfare** (trade wars, embargoes, the weekly league) and/or reintroduce missiles late-game priced as a deliberate sink (Albion's full-loot destruction is what keeps its economy alive).
- **Deep production chains at launch** — already correctly scoped out; SCBI's depth is 2 levels with time, not graph complexity, providing the difficulty. The doc's 2–3 step instinct is validated.

## 5. Risks the doc doesn't mention

1. **Poll brigading / city capture.** eRepublik has a documented "political takeover" meta (organized blocs out-voting locals); r/place coordination ran through off-platform Discords. An organized group *will* migrate into a small city and capture its poll. Mitigations: residency tenure before voting, vote-weight caps, migration cooldowns.
2. **Whale capture compounding.** v1's Warrior 2x already weights contribution; multiplier tiers ×10 make one payer = ten players in any contribution-weighted system. (Hence P0 #2.)
3. **Web platform retention.** Browser games have no install commitment and no push by default; D1 will look bad vs mobile benchmarks (median ~25-27% D1, ~7-8% D7). Web is viable (Poki: 625M players/yr; Melvor: ~$6.7M on Steam) but only with a re-engagement channel (P1 #9).
4. **The conversion math.** F2P converts ~1–5% of players, and whales (~1–2%) drive 50–70% of revenue. A single multiplier SKU is a thin monetization surface; the hybrid that's now standard is sub + consumable boosts + (later) pass. Converting v1's Warrior subscription into a permanent multiplier tier is the right instinct — keep both shapes (open question #4: "both", with the sub as the anchor).
5. **One cautionary counter-voice on clicker F2P:** Playsaurus walked away from F2P for Clicker Heroes 2 over addiction-exploitation concerns, citing players "in denial of their addiction." Worth one design conversation about where the line is, since the capped-meter design is explicitly a frustration-monetization loop.

## 6. Answers to the doc's open questions for Paul

1. **Missiles:** remove at MVP; bring conflict back later as economic warfare and/or late-game missiles priced as an economy sink. Don't delete the code.
2. **Vote weight:** capped contribution weight (e.g., votes per active day, or quadratic) — and **never multiplied by payment**. The evidence on purchasable governance is uniformly bad.
3. **Small cities:** fine as a difficulty choice **only with cheap migration** and gentle decay of inactive cities; add residency tenure so migration can't be weaponized for poll capture.
4. **Multiplier shape:** evolve the Warrior subscription into a permanent-multiplier membership *and* sell timed boosts; production-only, never votes.
5. **Market guardrails:** wide bands at launch used as anomaly/abuse detection (OGame precedent), transaction + listing fees as the real stabilizer (EVE precedent), explicit anti-multi enforcement. Plan to widen/remove bands, never tighten.

## 7. Revised MVP scope (if the P0 list is accepted)

Shared home city · 5–8 buildings · 4–6 resources · needs/happiness (happiness → income & click effectiveness; housing+materials → population) · **idle baseline production + clicks as active boost** · pay-then-click construction · market with fees + wide bands + flat delivery delay · construction poll with **capped, non-purchasable vote weight** · per-city feed/chat + contributor recognition · click meter + paid production multipliers · daily quests · sinks designed in. Defer: transport tiers, deep chains, seasons/pass, events tooling, prestige.
